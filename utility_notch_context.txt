==================================================
1. HIGH-LEVEL PRODUCT DIRECTION
==================================================

Project name:
- Utility Notch

Core idea:
- Native macOS notch-style utility workspace.
- Built only for macOS.
- Native Swift + SwiftUI.
- No Windows support.
- Menu bar utility app.
- No Dock icon.
- Separate Settings window.
- Floating NSPanel shell.
- Opens from notch-area interaction and acts as a modular container for small utilities.

Product identity direction chosen:
- Private
- Modular
- Professional utility workspace
- Fast
- Compact
- Apple-like
- More for devs and pros than for generic flashy consumer gimmicks

Important product rule:
- The panel should behave like a mini workspace, not a transient tooltip.
- It must stay stable during interaction.
- It should close reliably when idle and clicked away from, unless active interaction, active drag/drop, active conversion, timer-running state, or similar context says it should stay open.

Brand direction:
- Small elegant tools with strong privacy and clean UX.
- Core modules should solve fast desktop problems in under a few seconds.

- panel: NSPanel?

- hidePanel() animates alpha to zero, then orderOut via work item
