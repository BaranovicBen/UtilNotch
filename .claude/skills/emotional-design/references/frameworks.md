# Emotional Design Reference Frameworks

## Geneva Emotion Wheel (GEW 2.0) — Quadrant Map

| Quadrant | Control | Valence | Emotions to design for |
|---|---|---|---|
| I — Aspirational | High | Positive | Pride, Joy, Elation, Amusement |
| II — Threat Response | High | Negative | Anger, Contempt, Disgust, Scorn |
| III — Vulnerability | Low | Negative | Fear, Sadness, Guilt, Shame |
| IV — Receptive | Low | Positive | Relief, Interest, Wonder, Love |

**Design implication**: Map each screen/flow to a target quadrant. Onboarding targets Quadrant I (Joy, Amusement). Error states should aim for Quadrant IV (Relief, Interest) rather than leaving users in Quadrant III.

## Desmet-Hekkert Product Experience Framework

Three components must all be addressed for holistic emotional design:

1. **Aesthetic Experience** — Gratification of the senses. Does the product feel beautiful or pleasing to perceive?
2. **Experience of Meaning** — Cognitive assignment of personality and symbolic significance. Does the product say something about who the user is?
3. **Emotional Experience** — Discrete feelings (love, fascination, frustration) elicited by the product's appraised relational meaning.

A product that only addresses aesthetics will attract but not retain. One that only addresses meaning will resonate intellectually but not emotionally.

## The Hooked Model — Implementation Guide

| Phase | Design Element | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Trigger** (External) | Push notification, email, social prompt | "Your friend just completed a challenge" |
| **Trigger** (Internal) | Emotion cue (boredom, anxiety, FOMO) | App icon during idle moment |
| **Action** | Simplest behavior in anticipation of reward | One tap to open, swipe to refresh |
| **Variable Reward** | Tribe / Hunt / Self | New message, discovery, level up |
| **Investment** | User puts in data, time, social capital | Profile, preferences, followers |

**Warning signals for dopamine traps**:
- Celebratory animations on high-risk financial actions
- Infinite scroll with no natural stopping point
- Streak mechanics that create anxiety about breaking chains
- Notifications timed to exploit vulnerability windows (late night, post-rejection)
